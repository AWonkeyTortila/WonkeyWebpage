I fly

It’s two AM. I’ve packed my bags, and I’m ready to leave.

Mother and father usually argue.

But mother was worried that day. She tried not to cry. My father smiled and hugged me for once, But he knew it was hopeless.

I was their only son. My brother died young, And lost my sister in a car accident. And soon I’ll join them.

I headed up the road, And waited at the bus stop.

My friends wished me a good trip. They told me to write them back, And to tell them how New York is. But they’ll be disappointed.

The bus came, and I was all alone. I sobbed for a bit, And drank a bit of alcohol.

I thought of my loved ones, And I heard a voice in my head, It tried to stop me.

But there’s nothing it can do.

I got off the bus, and entered the air port. I’ll fly tonight, and fly for one last time.

I waited in the lobby, And I sobered up.

They’ll miss me, I thought, It’s not to late to go back. But they didn’t try and stop me.

Soon I’ll be in the sky, and amongst the stars. Tonight I’ll fly. I’ll fly, I’ll fly, Tonight I’ll fly.


It’s been so long, that I’ve forgotten your name… Hopefully I never forget you, however.
